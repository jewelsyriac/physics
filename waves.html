Introduction

A wave is a disturbance that travels through space and time, accompanied by a transfer of energy. A wave motion is produced due to harmonic oscillations of a series of points of the medium.

Types of Waves

Mechanical wave

Waves that require a material medium for propagation are called mechanical waves. Here waves are produced by periodic vibrations of the particles of the medium.

Example : Sound waves & Seismic waves

Electromagnetic waves

Waves which does not require a material medium for propagation are called electromagnetic waves. So they can travel through vaccum. In electromagnetic waves, electric & magnetic fields oscillates perpendicular to direction of propagation of wave.

Examples: Light waves, Radio waves, X-rays etc..

According to the direction of vibration of the particles of medium with respect to direction of propagation of waves are classified into two types

Transverse waves.

If the direction of vibration of the particles of medium is perpendicular to direction of propagation of wave, such waves are called transverse waves.

Example : light waves and water waves

Longitudinal waves.

If the direction of vibration of the particles of medium is parallel to direction of propagation of wave, such waves are called longitudinal waves.

Example : sound waves and ultrasonic waves

Properties of Waves

Wavelength

Wavelength is a measure of the distance between two consecutive peaks (or troughs) of a wave. Generally wavelength can be defined as distance between two consecutive points of a wave that are in phase.

Frequency

Frequency of a wave is the number of oscillations or vibrationsof particles of medium (or wave itself) per unit time.

Amplitude

Amplitude is maximum displacement of a particle from equilibrium point in a wave (keep in mind that wave is propagation of vibration/oscillation of this particles in a medium). It is the distance between the highest point (crest) or the lowest point (trough) of a wave and the equilibrium or rest position.

Wave velocity

Wave velocity is a measure of the speed at which a wave travels through a medium and depends on the properties of the medium and the wavelength and frequency of the wave.

Particle velocity

particle velocity of a wave is the velocity of the individual particles of the medium oscillates. It varies depending on the position in the medium, it's different from the wave velocity which is the speed at which the wave itself travels.

Initial phase

Initial phase of a wave is the starting point or position of a wave cycle at a given time, it's measured in degrees and it gives the position of a point on the waveform in relation to a reference point.

Wave equation

A single pulse or wave moving in positive x direction can be represented as,

$$u(x,t)=f(x-vt)\;-->(eqn1)$$

where u is the vertical displacement of particle in wave and f is the function representing shape of the waveform.

Differentiating equation (1) w.r.t to x twice,

$$\frac{\partial^u}{\partial x^2}=f^{''}(x-vt) \; ---eqn(2)$$

Differentiating equation (1) w.r.t t twice

$$\frac{\partial^u}{\partial t^2}=v^2 \;f^{''}(x-vt)\;---eqn(3)$$

substituting value of \(f^{''}(x-vt)\) from equation(2) in equation(3),

$$\frac{\partial^u}{\partial t^2} =v^2 \; \frac{\partial^u}{\partial x^2} $$

rearranging,

$$\boxed{\frac{\partial^u}{\partial x^2} =\frac{1}{v^2} \; \frac{\partial^u}{\partial t^2}} $$

Above equation is called one dimensional wave equation.

Conclusion

Waves are an important concept in physics and are found in a wide range of natural and man-made systems. Understanding waves allows us to predict and control the behavior of these systems, and has numerous practical applications in fields such as communication, transportation, and technology.

Numerical questions.

  1. The equation of transverse wave travelling along a stretched string is given by, $$y(x,t) = 30 sin ( 0.5x - 6t + \pi ) $$ where y and x are in cm and t is in second. Find the amplitude, frequency, velocity, wavelength, wave number and initial phase of the wave.

    Answer:

    Given equation of wave is

    $$y(x,t) = 30 sin ( 0.5x - 6t + \pi ) $$ The general equation of wave is $$y(x,t) = A sin ( kx \pm \omega t + \phi ) $$ By comparing both the equations, we can find the values of the parameters:

    Amplitude A = 30 cm

    Wave number, k = 0.5 rad/cm

    Angular frequency, \( \omega \) = 6 rad/s

    Frequency, $$f = \frac{\omega}{2 \pi } = \frac{6}{2 \pi } = 0.96 Hz $$ Wavelength, $$\lambda = \frac{2 \pi }{k} = \frac{2 \pi }{0.5} = 4 \pi \text{ cm}$$ Velocity of wave, $$v = \frac{\omega}{k}=\frac{6}{0.5} = 12 \text{ cm/s} $$

    Initial phase,

    $$ \phi = \pi \;radians$$ So, amplitude of the wave is 30 cm, frequency of the wave is 0.96 Hz, wavelength of the wave is 4 \(\pi\) cm, velocity of the wave is 12 cm/s and initial phase of the wave is \( \pi \) radians.
  2. The equation of a transverse wave traveling along a stretched string is given by: $$y(x,t) = 25 sin ( 0.7x + 8t + \frac{\pi}{4}) $$ where y and x are in cm and t is in seconds. Find the amplitude, frequency, wavelength, wave number, velocity, and initial phase of the wave.

    Answer:

    Given equation of wave is

    $$y(x,t) = 25 sin ( 0.7x + 8t + \frac{\pi}{4}) $$ The general equation of wave is $$y(x,t) = A sin ( kx \pm \omega t + \phi ) $$ By comparing both the equations, we can find the values of the parameters:

    Amplitude A = 25 cm

    Wave number, k = 0.7 rad/cm

    Angular frequency, \( \omega \) = 8 rad/s

    Frequency, $$f = \frac{\omega}{2 \pi } = \frac{8}{2 \pi } = 1.27 Hz $$ Wavelength, $$\lambda = \frac{2 \pi }{k} = \frac{2 \pi }{0.7} = 2.857 \text{ cm}$$ Velocity of wave, $$v = \frac{\omega}{k}=\frac{8}{0.7} = 11.428 \text{ cm/s} $$ Initial phase, $$ \phi = \frac{\pi}{4} \;radians$$ So, amplitude of the wave is 25 cm, frequency of the wave is 1.27 Hz, wavelength of the wave is 2.857 cm, wave number is 0.7 rad/cm, velocity of the wave is 11.428 cm/s and initial phase of the wave is \( \frac{\pi}{4} \) radians.
  3. The equation of a transverse wave traveling along a stretched string is given by: $$y(x,t) = 20 cos ( 0.6x - 7t ) $$ where y and x are in cm and t is in seconds. Find the amplitude, frequency, wavelength, wave number, velocity, and initial phase of the wave.

    Answer:

    Given equation of wave is

    $$y(x,t) = 20 cos ( 0.6x - 7t ) $$ The general equation of wave is $$y(x,t) = A cos ( kx - \omega t + \phi ) $$ By comparing both the equations, we can find the values of the parameters:

    Amplitude A = 20 cm

    Wave number, k = 0.6 rad/cm

    Angular frequency, \( \omega \) = 7 rad/s

    Frequency, $$f = \frac{\omega}{2 \pi } = \frac{7}{2 \pi } = 1.11 Hz $$ Wavelength, $$\lambda = \frac{2 \pi }{k} = \frac{2 \pi }{0.6} = 3.333 \text{ cm}$$ Velocity of wave, $$v = \frac{\omega}{k}=\frac{7}{0.6} = 11.666 \text{ cm/s} $$ Initial phase, $$ \phi = 0 \;radians$$ So, amplitude of the wave is 20 cm, frequency of the wave is 1.11 Hz, wavelength of the wave is 3.333 cm, wave number is 0.6 rad/cm, velocity of the wave is 11.666 cm/s and initial phase of the wave is 0 radians.
  4. The equation of a transverse wave moving through a medium is given by: $$y(x,t) = 15 sin ( 0.8x + 9t - \frac{\pi}{3}) $$ where y and x are in cm and t is in seconds. Determine the amplitude, frequency, wavelength, wave number, velocity, and initial phase of the wave.

    Answer:

    Given equation of wave is

    $$y(x,t) = 15 sin ( 0.8x + 9t - \frac{\pi}{3}) $$ The general equation of a transverse wave is $$y(x,t) = A sin ( kx + \omega t + \phi ) $$

    By comparing both the equations, we can find the values of the parameters:

    Amplitude A = 15 cm

    Wave number, k = 0.8 rad/cm

    Angular frequency, \( \omega \) = 9 rad/s

    Frequency, $$f = \frac{\omega}{2 \pi } = \frac{9}{2 \pi } = 1.43 Hz $$ Wavelength, $$\lambda = \frac{2 \pi }{k} = \frac{2 \pi }{0.8} = 2.5 \text{ cm}$$ Velocity of wave, $$v = \frac{\omega}{k}=\frac{9}{0.8} = 11.25 \text{ cm/s} $$ Initial phase, $$ \phi = - \frac{\pi}{3} \;radians$$ So, amplitude of the wave is 15 cm, frequency of the wave is 1.43 Hz, wavelength of the wave is 2.5 cm, wave number is 0.8 rad/cm, velocity of the wave is 11.25 cm/s and initial phase of the wave is \(-\frac{\pi}{3}\) radians.
